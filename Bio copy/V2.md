Hi, I'm Simon Demarmels.

Over the past years, I've worked with scale-ups and SMEs at the intersection of strategy, operations, and capital. The work spans fundraising, M&A, commercial diligence, go-to-market, and the broader strategic calls underneath. Over time, my focus has drifted from highly scalable software businesses to science-based and technically complex ones. These are companies built on real R&D, long development cycles, and uncertain commercialisation paths. Beautiful problems to work on, but rarely easy to price.

That distance between tangible progress and what an investor can confidently underwrite is what led to Evolute.

In practice, the work means sitting close to founders and management teams to figure out what actually drives value, and making the calls that follow. Positioning. What to prioritise and what to kill. How to sharpen go-to-market without overreaching. How to scale internal processes and teams without bloating cost. How to align people across functions. And how to ground all of it in financial reality and a capital strategy that respects the opportunity cost of every euro raised.

Building Evolute itself has been a version of the same exercise. Setting strategy, shaping operations, hiring, and executing. Every decision priced in real consequence, financial and reputational.

Most of the time, the work is less about applying a playbook and more about structured experimentation, disciplined execution, and judgment under uncertainty. Which is really just capital allocation under another name. That is where I tend to do my best work.
